    Esophageal Cancer in Garissa County

    R SPSS Statistical Modeling

    Description

    A comprehensive study examining sociodemographic and behavioral factors associated with esophageal cancer in Garissa County, Kenya. The research included patient medical record analysis, and statistical modeling to identify key risk factors.

    Interpretation

    The study revealed significant associations between esophageal cancer and tobacco use (p = 0.022), hypertension (p = 0.025), diabetes mellitus (p = 0.028) and HIV status (p = 0.029). Additionally, a family history of cancer was associated with esophageal cancer (p = 0.009). There was no significant association between sociodemographic factors such as age, marital status, or education level. However, the majority of the study participants lacked formal education.

    Breast Cancer Subtypes in Meru County

    R Data Visualization

    Description

    An analysis of sociodemographic and clinical correlates of breast cancer subtypes in Meru County, Kenya. The project involved collecting and analyzing patient data to identify patterns and correlations between cancer subtypes and various factors.

    Interpretation

    Significant associations were observed between breast cancer subtypes and occupation with farmers being the most represented occupational group (p = 0.002), HER2 status (p < 0.001), and the Ki-67 index (p=0 .044). Age, BMI, marital status, and receptor status (ER, PR) were not statistically associated with subtypes.

    Lung Cancer Risk Factors

    R Machine Learning Statistical Analysis

    Description

    Research focused on determining symptom interrelations and behavioral correlates of lung cancer risk. The study employed advanced statistical methods to analyze complex relationships between symptoms, behaviors, and cancer risk.

    Interpretation

    In my analysis of lung cancer data, I found that while smoking and certain lifestyle habits were associated with cancer risk, age (average difference, p = 0.18) and other demographic factors were less significant. Interestingly, predictive modeling revealed that allergy history and alcohol use were stronger indicators of lung cancer than smoking itself, with symptoms like wheezing and swallowing difficulties also contributing to risk patterns. Symptom clusters such as chest pain and shortness of breath often occurred together, suggesting underlying links, while smoking and alcohol appeared to influence cancer risk independently.     Coffee Supply Chain Optimization

    Excel Data Analysis

    Description

    An analysis of coffee supply chain data focusing on optimizing yield, enumerator performance, and farm practices. The project involved processing large datasets to identify efficiency improvements and best practices across the supply chain.

    Interpretation

    Limmu recorded the highest yield (438.23), over 120 units above Sidama B (312.25), while Yirgachefe trailed at 195.26, pointing to region-specific challenges. Yirgachefe and Sidama B favored organic fertilizer and intercropping, whereas Limmu led in disease-resistant varieties. Pruning was dominated by desuckering (428) and branch removal (419), with topping (287) ranking third, while other methods were minimal. Interestingly, higher adoption of sustainable practices did not always translate into higher yields, suggesting other influential factors.
